Arcane Ascension — Privacy Policy

Effective date: July 2, 2026 · Last updated: July 20, 2026

This privacy policy describes how Arcane Ascension (“the app”, “we”) handles information when you play the game on Android, iOS, or the web. Arcane Ascension is developed by Ian Murray (“the developer”).

The short version: the game has no accounts and never asks for your name, email, or contact details. Your game progress stays on your device. The app uses Google services for anonymous usage analytics, crash reporting, and — on Android and iOS only — optional rewarded ads.

Information we collect

We do not collect any information that directly identifies you. The app includes the following third-party services, which collect limited data automatically:

Google Analytics for Firebase

Collects anonymous usage data so we can understand how the game is played and improve it — for example: matches started and finished, game settings used, spells cast, feature usage, session length, approximate region (derived from IP address, which is not retained), device model, and operating system version. This data is associated with a randomly generated app-instance identifier, not with you.

Firebase Crashlytics

Collects crash reports and diagnostics (stack traces, device state at the time of a crash, device model, OS version) so we can find and fix bugs.

Google AdMob (Android and iOS only)

The mobile app can show an optional, rewarded video ad — only when you choose to watch one. AdMob may collect device advertising identifiers and ad-interaction data to serve and measure ads.

  • In the European Economic Area, the UK, and Switzerland: a consent form is shown before any ads are requested. If you do not consent to personalized ads, ads are either not shown or shown on a non-personalized basis.
  • On iOS: personalized ads are only enabled if you allow tracking via Apple’s App Tracking Transparency prompt. If you decline, ads are non-personalized.
  • The web version of the game shows no ads.

Cookies and similar technologies (web version)

The web version of the game does not use cookies for advertising. It may use a small number of first-party Google Analytics cookies (such as _ga) solely for the anonymous usage measurement described above, plus your browser’s local storage for game progress and settings, which is strictly necessary for the game to function and never leaves your device.

  • In the European Economic Area, the UK, and Switzerland: no analytics cookies are set unless you accept the consent prompt shown the first time you open the game. If you decline (or before you answer), the game sends only anonymous, cookieless measurement signals via Google Consent Mode.
  • Elsewhere: analytics cookies are used by default; you can turn them off at any time with the “Share anonymous usage data” toggle in the game’s settings.

Information stored on your device

Game progress (unlocks, statistics, achievements, in-progress matches, settings) is stored locally on your device and, on Android, may be backed up via Google Block Store so progress can transfer to a new device. We cannot access this data; it never reaches our servers.

How we use information

  • To understand feature usage and improve the game
  • To find and fix crashes and bugs
  • To serve and measure the optional rewarded ads (mobile only)
  • To measure aggregate game balance (e.g., which spells are over- or under-used)

We do not sell your data. We do not use the data for any purpose other than those listed above.

Data sharing

Data collected by the services above is processed by Google LLC under their own privacy policies:

  • Google Privacy Policy
  • How Google uses data from partner apps
  • AdMob data usage

We do not share data with anyone else.

Data retention

Analytics and crash data are retained by Google Firebase per their standard retention controls (user-level analytics data is retained for a limited period, then aggregated). Local game data remains on your device until you delete the app or reset progress in the app’s settings.

Your rights and choices

  • Analytics consent: in regions with consent laws (EEA/UK/CH), the game asks once before collecting any analytics. Everyone can turn “Share anonymous usage data” on or off in the game’s settings at any time; turning it off disables analytics and crash reporting for your device.
  • Ads consent (EEA/UK/CH): you can change your ads consent choice at any time from the game’s settings.
  • iOS tracking: iOS Settings → Privacy & Security → Tracking.
  • Android ads personalization: Android Settings → Privacy → Ads (reset or delete your advertising ID).
  • Deletion: deleting the app removes all locally stored game data. Because analytics data is not tied to your identity, we cannot look up or delete individual records on request — but you can ask us to delete data associated with your app instance if you provide the app-instance ID.
  • Depending on your jurisdiction (e.g., GDPR in the EU, CCPA/CPRA in California), you may have additional rights such as access, correction, deletion, and objection to processing. Contact us to exercise them.

Children

Arcane Ascension is not directed at children under 13 (or the equivalent minimum age in your jurisdiction), and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children. Ads served through AdMob use Google’s standard protections; if you believe a child has provided personal information through the app, contact us and we will address it.

Security

The app makes no network requests other than those made by the Google services described above, which use encrypted (HTTPS) connections. Locally stored progress uses your device’s standard app storage protections (and the platform keychain/keystore where applicable).

Changes to this policy

If we make material changes, we will update this page and the effective date above. Continued use of the app after changes take effect constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.
